WDD 130

Your Personal Site Content

Activity Instructions

Estimated Time: 60 minutes.

This week, we will do an activity to help us generate the content we will need to complete our personal site.

Information about your personal site that might be helpful to know as you gather content.

By the end of this course, these objectives must be met:

  1. The personal site must consist of a home page and at least two child pages.
  2. The website must be hosted on the web.
  3. Each page of your website must satisfy the following requirements:
    1. Significant textual content consisting of appropriate content for your audience so that they can understand the meaning and message of this site.
    2. At least one image that relates to the content of the page.
    3. Working links to your two child pages.
    4. Be laid out and styled according to your site plan wireframes.
    5. Be visually appealing and easily readable.

Activity Directions

Over that last couple of weeks, you were asked to decide on a topic for your personal website and create an elevator pitch 'selling' your idea. You should also have thought about your intended audience. This week, begin by reviewing your pitch and audience. Then think about three topics your site will cover. Write those three topics down.

  1. Brainstorm and Write!

    With your topics and audience in mind, please write at least 600 words on your website idea providing text content that your audience may want to read or get from your website. You can just freewrite any pertinent information about your website idea following good paragraph and sentence structure, thought processes, and so on. This content will be a rough draft in this week but will be refined in subsequent weeks to become what the users will be reading from your website. You may also consider answering some or all of the following questions about your website idea:

    • Why will people come to your site?
    • What questions will your visitors expect your site to answer for them?
    • What types of activities will visitors want to complete on your site?
    • What information is necessary to complete any actions your site offers?
    • Why will your visitors come to your site instead of another to get this information or complete the actions?

  2. Organize

    1. Organize your paragraphs so there is "one idea per paragraph". This may involve breaking up or otherwise editing paragraphs.
    2. Name each paragraph by typing and bolding a paragraph-topic name for each paragraph. This process can help you determine link names and additional topics that will be in your website.
    3. Organize your paragraphs by collecting and moving all the paragraphs that are related and alike into respective areas along with their paragraph-topic names. For example, you might have multiple paragraphs in your document pertaining to benefits of your website idea. You should move all of those paragraphs (leaving the paragraph name) into the same area of your document.
    4. Finally, consider each set of related paragraphs. Identify a representative "main-topic" title for each group. Each of these groups could become a page on your site. The "main-topic" label you choose would become the link text for the page in the main navigation. Remember that for our site we will have at least three pages. So you should pick at 2–3 of your main-topics that you will use for your site. One for the home page, and two for child pages.

  3. Images

    Images play a very large role in how we view and feel about websites. You should put some thought into the images you will need to back up and enhance the content you generated above.

    We covered good image design practices and optimizing images in Week 3 in Step 8 of Creating HTML for the Home Page. Visit the link if you need a refresher.

    For each of your pages, find 2–3 images that could be used to enhance the content of that page. These can be images that you create yourself or images that you have found. If you find the images online, make sure to honor copyright. Do not use images that are not stated as free to use. Here are a couple of good sources for good, high quality, free stock images and icons.

    Make sure to optimize your images!

    • Pexels.com
    • Unsplash
    • Canva
    • Flaticon.com for free icons
    • Freepik

    Optimize your chosen images using something like picresize.com and add your images to your site plan for each page.

  4. Update site plan

    Update the content section of your site plan with the "main-topic" names you have chosen to replace 'Page 2' and 'Page 3'.

    Where is has 'Written copy for Page # here', you will be replacing the text and [] square brackets with your own text content for that page.

    Replace the '#' as the value for the src for the images with your own image paths. These images should be optimized and in your images folder or your project so they show up in the site plan.

  5. Upload to I-Learn

    Submit your site plan URL, which includes all your content images and text, to I-Learn.
