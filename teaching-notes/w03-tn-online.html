Week 03: Creating the Home page

Before the Lesson Begins

  • This week, the students will write about 90% of the HTML for the home page for the rafting site. 

    W03 Quiz: Preparation

  • Remind students that they should finished the preparation videos and/or reading assignment and quiz at the first of the week. This ensures they are ready to jump into the hands-on exercises.
  • Even if some 'Ponder' assignments are not graded, encourage the students to carefully complete them. They will need the skills covered for 'Prove' assignments.

During the Lesson

    W03 Classes and ID Practice

  • Students can practice with classes and ids by adding to the web page they have practiced with in the last few weeks by following this Classes and IDs Practice Example

    W03 Working with Wireframes

  • The Site plan template contains a wireframe that we will be using this week.  Stress two things to the students: first, the assignment will make much more sense if they refer to the wireframe often while reading the instructions, and second their page will NOT look like the wireframe at the end of this week. We will have all of the content represented there on our page, but it will be weeks before our page looks just like the wireframe. 
  • With this assignment, the student practice taking a wireframe and making HTML code using the wireframe. They will be using the wireframe from dribbble.com They will view this follow-up video to the one they watch in preparation explaining what you might do in class after studying the groupings of a wireframe:

    W03 Troubleshooting

  • The 'Troubleshooting with Validation' is a good exercise for students to introduce them to validation.

    W03 Frontend Developer

  • The Web Frontend Developer information is our first in a series of web career exploration information. You can require a report from your students if you want.

    W03 Assignment: HTML for the Home Page

  • Students will be stepped through adding the HTML to their home page with this assignment. Stress to them that they must follow the directions carefully. They are given code many times in these type of assignments but sometimes (especially in future weeks) they are required to come up with the code on their own and if they are not reading carefully they will miss these steps.
  • Students will also add images to their home page. They are given images to use, but are also welcome to add their own images. If they want different images I would refer them to the collection of rafting images that we have already gathered for this course at: Alternative small, medium, and larger rafting iamges and logos They are already optimized.
  • Make sure to stress the importance of completing the Troubleshooting and Wireframe activities as well. They are ungraded, but important that they understand.
  • For some students using Mac computers, the hero.png images causes issues because of its size. They will get a 400 error when they go to push that image. (...RPC failed; HTTP 400 curl 22...) If that happens run this command on their terminal to avoid it in the future: git config --global http.postBuffer 157286400
  • Help the students understand constructive versus destructive criticism. The criticism needs to be valid and concrete.
  • Make sure you are available to students through office hours and make responses within 24 hours.
